Our Perspective

Over the last 30 years, the Saudi pharmaceutical industry has made revolutionary contributions to health care. The Saudi pharmaceutical industry now faces the challenges of drug manufacturing, increasingly competitive markets, and uncertain policy and regulatory environments. Regardless of ongoing medications need related to numerous diseases, the Saudi pharmaceutical sectors can no longer depend on their pricing power to achieve profitable growth.

The net result of ongoing cost pressures and a precipitous decline in R&D productivity will be an unprecedented decline in the share of the overall healthcare profit pool captured by the largest research-based pharmaceutical companies.

Lower-margin pharmaceutical sectors, like generic manufacturers in the Kingdome of Saudi Arabia, will increase their share. The critical question for the Saudi pharmaceutical industry is how to evolve their business model in order to thrive in a world of shifting profit pools by focusing on the high-quality outcomes by R&D rather than by simply generating more inputs, more products, more procedures and ultimately more cost to the payers.
